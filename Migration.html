5. Migration

5.1. Migration as a component of population change

Movements of Populations:

  • Source - place that migrants come from
  • Destination - where migrants go
  • Interational Migration - when an individual or group of people cross an international border for more than 1 year
    • Immigrant - someone who moves into a country
    • Emigrant - someone who leaves their country
  • Internal Migration - when people move from one place to another inside a country
  • Net Migration - difference between in-movements(immigrants) and out-movements(emigrants)
  • Migration stream - the common route that migrants take to get from a source to a destination
    • Counter stream - reverse of the migation stream (people return home). Reduced volume of poeple
  • Distance-Decay - as the distance increases (between source and destination), the number of migrants reduces
    •  
  • Permanent Migration: Complete transfer of residence, often with the entire family
    • Factors:
      • Employment opportunites (economic factor)
      • Family reunification (social factor)
      • Escape due to fear of death, for example in war areas (political factor)
  • Semi-permanent Migration: Long-term stay in new location, but with the possibility or intention of returning back home (source)
    • Factors:
      • Temporary work contract (economic)
      • Education (social)
      • Extended travel (social)
  • Temporary Migration: Short-term movement, with intention of returning home after a limited period
    • Factors:
      • Seasonal labor migration (economic)
      • Short-term educational courses (social)
      • Tourism/travel (social)
    • Typically individual rather than family migration
  • Involuntary vs Voluntary Migration:
    • Voluntary - choose to move for better standard of living (better jobs and higher wages). Most of the time for economic reasons.
    • Involuntary - forced to move to avoid extreme hardship, persecution, or death. These people are usually refugees

Causes of Migration:

  • 5 types of migration:
    • Primitive - moving for physical farming factors
    • Forced - slave trade, Chernobyl, natural disaster
    • Impelled - under preceived threat
    • Free - not forced
    • Mass - high magnitude
  • Push Factors: reasons that encourage people to move away from source
    • Civil wars
    • Government corruption
    • Government corruption
    • Natural Disasters / climate condition
    • Poor Employment and low income
    • Bad education
    • Bad healthcare
  • Pull Factors: positive aspects about the destination
    • Job prospects and high income promise
    • Higher living standard
    • More entertainments
    • Better education and healthcare
    • Stabler government
  • Chain Mirgation - after a few migrants leave, a chain reaction is set off, and many more follow
  • Relay Mirgation - at different stages in the family life cycle, different people migrate to improve their financial situation
  • Step Migration - rural migrants progressively step/move to bigger settlements (from rural town, to small town, to small city, to larger city etc...
